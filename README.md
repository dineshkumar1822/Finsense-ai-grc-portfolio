# FinSense AI — GRC & AI Governance Portfolio Project

A self-initiated consulting simulation demonstrating end-to-end GRC delivery — combining an ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Information Security Management System (ISMS) gap assessment with an AI governance review aligned to the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0).

---

## Project Overview

**FinSense AI Technologies Pvt. Ltd.** is a fictional 50-person fintech startup based in Bangalore, operating an AI-powered loan origination and decisioning platform for personal and MSME loans. The company processes sensitive personal and financial data (Aadhaar, PAN, bank statements, credit scores) and has no formally established ISMS or AI governance framework.

This project simulates a real GRC consulting engagement — playing the role of an external consultant assessing FinSense AI's information security and AI governance posture, identifying gaps, and producing a prioritised remediation roadmap.

**Why this scenario:** Fintech + AI lending sits at the intersection of two of the fastest-growing GRC specialisations in India — traditional information security compliance and emerging AI governance regulation (EU AI Act, India DPDP Act 2023, RBI Digital Lending Guidelines).

---

## Frameworks Applied

| Framework | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **ISO/IEC 27001:2022** | Information Security Management System — gap assessment across all 93 Annex A controls |
| **NIST AI RMF 1.0** | AI-specific risk governance — assessed across GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE |
| **EU AI Act** | Regulatory baseline — loan approval AI classified as High Risk (Annex III) |
| **India DPDP Act 2023** | Personal data protection obligations for Indian data subjects |

## Key Findings (Summary)

- **31% ISO 27001 Annex A compliance** — FinSense AI has no formally established ISMS
- **No human oversight** of AI loan decisions — a mandatory requirement under EU AI Act Article 14 for High Risk AI systems
- **No bias testing** conducted on the AI model despite training on historical lending data with known discrimination risk
- **Shared administrative access** across all developers with no MFA or segregation of duties
- **No incident response or breach notification procedure** — direct exposure under India's DPDP Act 2023

---

## Methodology

1. **Company Profile Development** — defined FinSense AI's business context, data assets, technology environment, and regulatory obligations
2. **ISO 27001 Gap Assessment** — evaluated all 93 Annex A controls against the company profile across Organisational, People, Physical, and Technological domains
3. **Risk Register Development** — applied asset-threat-vulnerability methodology to identify and score 15+ risks using a 5x5 likelihood/impact matrix
4. **NIST AI RMF Assessment** — assessed the AI loan model across GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE functions
5. **Remediation Roadmap** — consolidated all findings into a phased 6-month action plan aligned to ISO 27001 certification readiness
